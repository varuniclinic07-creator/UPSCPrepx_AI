Protection of Life and Personal Liberty

Article 21 Fundamental Rights

verified Core Mandate

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law. This right is available to both citizens and non-citizens.

Key Interpretations

  • Menaka Gandhi Case (1978): The Supreme Court introduced the concept of "Due Process of Law". The procedure must be fair, just, and reasonable, not arbitrary.
  • Right to Privacy: Declared as an intrinsic part of Article 21 in the K.S. Puttaswamy Judgment (2017).
  • Right to Health & Environment: Expanded scope includes right to clean environment, health, and livelihood.

school Prelims Byte

Article 21 cannot be suspended even during a National Emergency (Article 359), following the 44th Amendment Act, 1978.
